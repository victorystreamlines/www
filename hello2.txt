Kuwait: A Small Nation with a Rich Heritage

Kuwait is a small but wealthy country located at the tip of the Persian Gulf in the Middle East. Bordered by Iraq to the north and Saudi Arabia to the south, this nation covers approximately 17,818 square kilometers, making it one of the smallest countries in the world.

History and Independence

Kuwait has a rich history dating back thousands of years, serving as a trading hub between Mesopotamia and coastal civilizations. The modern state of Kuwait was founded in the 18th century by the Al-Sabah family, who continue to rule today. Kuwait gained independence from British protection on June 19, 1961, and this date is celebrated annually as National Day.

Economy and Oil

The discovery of oil in 1938 transformed Kuwait from a modest trading and pearl-diving economy into one of the wealthiest nations per capita in the world. Oil reserves account for nearly 95% of export revenues and about 90% of government income. The Kuwait Petroleum Corporation manages the country's vast oil resources, and the nation has invested heavily in sovereign wealth funds to secure its financial future.

Culture and Society

Kuwaiti culture blends traditional Arab and Islamic values with modern influences. The country is known for its hospitality, traditional music, and distinctive architecture. The Kuwait Towers, an iconic landmark, symbolize the nation's modernization while respecting its heritage.

Despite its small size, Kuwait has made significant contributions to arts, literature, and regional diplomacy, often serving as a mediator in Gulf affairs.
