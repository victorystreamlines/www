Economic Recovery and Growth

Kuwait's economy is recovering in 2025 after two years of recession!
IMF Report - April 2025
Economic Growth Returns to Positive Territory
The International Monetary Fund projects Kuwait's economy to grow by 2.6% in 2025, after contracting 2.6% in 2024. The recovery is driven by higher oil production following the unwinding of OPEC+ cuts and resilient non-oil sector performance.
National Bank of Kuwait - July 2025
First Quarter Growth Recorded
The economy returned to positive territory in Q1 2025, recording a 1% year-on-year increase following seven consecutive quarters of contraction. The non-oil economy continued to expand with momentum in manufacturing, real estate, and transportation.

Impact of Regional Conflicts

Regional Analysis - 2025
Strait of Hormuz and Oil Trade
The ongoing regional tensions have affected global oil supply chains, with the Strait of Hormuz - a crucial trade artery for Kuwait and other Gulf producers - experiencing disruptions. However, Kuwait has maintained stable production levels and is benefiting from higher oil prices.
Economic Impact Assessment
Resilience Amid Geopolitical Challenges
Despite regional conflicts, Kuwait's economy has shown remarkable resilience. The country's strong financial reserves and diversified economic base have helped mitigate external shocks. The non-oil sector has been particularly robust, providing stability during volatile periods.
Kuwait's economy continues to grow despite regional challenges!

Budget and Fiscal Performance

Ministry of Finance - April 2025
Budget Deficit Better Than Expected
Kuwait recorded an actual budget deficit of 1.06 billion dinars ($3.46 billion) for 2024-2025, significantly lower than the projected shortfall of 5.6 billion dinars. Total revenues reached 22.06 billion dinars, surpassing estimates, with 19.36 billion dinars from oil income.

Fiscal Performance Metrics

Oil Revenue: 87.7% of Total Revenue
Deficit Reduction: 81% Better Than Forecast
Revenue Achievement: 117% of Target

Economic Reforms and Future Outlook

March 2025
Historic Public Debt Law Enacted
Kuwait enacted a new Public Debt Law enabling the government to issue debt for the first time in almost a decade. The law allows issuance of up to 30 billion Kuwaiti dinars ($98 billion) in debt instruments with maturities up to 50 years, marking Kuwait's return to international debt markets after an eight-year hiatus.
IMF Assessment
Accelerating Economic Diversification
The IMF emphasizes the need for accelerated reform implementation to promote economic diversification, enhance competitiveness, and boost non-oil growth. Kuwaiti banks have maintained strong capital and liquidity buffers, while non-performing loans remain low.
